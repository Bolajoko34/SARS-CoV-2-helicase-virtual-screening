# SARS-CoV-2-helicase-virtual-screening
Status: Completed | Undergraduate Thesis Project

## Background
SARS-CoV-2, the virus responsible for COVID-19, has claimed millions of lives and created an urgent need for effective antiviral drugs. The virus is a positive-sense single-stranded RNA virus belonging to the family Coronaviridae. Among all proteins encoded by the virus, helicase is a fundamental protein for viral replication and represents a strong antiviral target.

## Objective
To identify potential inhibitors of the SARS-CoV-2 helicase protein by performing virtual screening of chemical compounds against the ANP-PNP binding site using structure-based drug design.

## Method
Screened 1,176 chemical compounds against the ANP-PNP binding site of the SARS-CoV-2 helicase protein using GOLD docking software. Compounds were ranked by GOLD fitness scores and binding interaction metrics including hydrogen bond and Van der Waals contributions.

## Key Findings

1,176 compounds screened in total
49 compounds shortlisted for detailed analysis
3 top hits showed significant inhibition of the ANP-PNP binding site
Compound252 identified as the lead candidate with a GOLD score of 88.16
Best GOLD score recorded: 125.75
Average GOLD score across screened compounds: 77.91
Lead compounds showed stronger docking performance compared to control inhibitors
